## üîÑ Inverse Search - Start With Answer, Find Questions

**Concept:** Reverse the exploration process - start with a conclusion and discover all the questions that lead there.

**Features:**
- Input: "Panpsychism" (or any concept/conclusion)
- Output: AI generates multiple question paths that lead to this conclusion:
  - Path 1: "What is consciousness?" ‚Üí "Can consciousness be fundamental?" ‚Üí "Panpsychism"
  - Path 2: "What are qualia?" ‚Üí "How does matter generate experience?" ‚Üí "Panpsychism"
  - Path 3: "What is the hard problem?" ‚Üí "What if consciousness isn't emergent?" ‚Üí "Panpsychism"
- Shows you the REASONING TREE, not just the conclusion
- Reveals multiple epistemological paths
- Identifies weakest links in each chain of reasoning
- Compare paths by strength of evidence
- Shows you questions you SHOULD have asked

**Why it matters:**
- Exposes blind spots in your thinking
- Helps you understand how others reached different conclusions
- Reveals hidden assumptions
- Great for:
  - Understanding opposing viewpoints
  - Checking your own reasoning
  - Learning methodology
  - Reverse-engineering expertise
- Makes biases visible

**Use cases:**
- "How did flat-earthers reach this conclusion?" (reveals the reasoning errors)
- "How do experts think about climate change?" (shows expert reasoning paths)
- "What questions lead to Buddhism?" (explores philosophical pathways)
- Teaching critical thinking in schools

**Tech stack:**
- Custom prompt engineering for path generation
- Graph algorithms to find optimal paths
- Evidence strength scoring
- Visualization of multiple parallel paths

## ü§î Socratic Mode - AI Only Asks Questions

**Concept:** Instead of giving answers, the AI only asks you questions. Forces you to think through topics yourself.

**Features:**
- AI never provides information, only asks questions
- Responds to your answers with deeper questions
- Uses the Socratic method systematically
- Your answers become nodes in the graph
- AI detects:
  - Circular reasoning
  - Gaps in understanding
  - Unsupported assumptions
  - Contradictions in your thinking
- Difficulty levels:
  - Gentle: Leading questions
  - Moderate: Challenging questions
  - Brutal: Actively tries to find holes
- Progress tracking: "You've answered 15 questions, reached 3 insights, found 2 contradictions"
- Can save your thought process and review later

**Example dialogue:**
```
User: "I want to understand consciousness"
AI: "What do you mean by consciousness?"
User: "Like, awareness"
AI: "Is awareness the same as consciousness, or are they different?"
User: "I guess they could be different..."
AI: "What would make them different?"
User: "Well, awareness might be more basic..."
AI: "What's the simplest thing that could be aware?"
```

**Why it matters:**
- This is the Feynman technique automated
- Exposes what you don't actually understand
- Builds genuine understanding, not memorization
- Great for:
  - Students preparing for exams
  - Researchers clarifying their thinking
  - Writers developing arguments
  - Therapists (exploring emotions/patterns)
  - Self-reflection and metacognition
- Forces active learning

**Tech stack:**
- Custom system prompts for Socratic questioning
- Logic engine to detect contradictions
- NLP for analyzing response quality
- Graph visualization of thought progression

## üíæ The Memex Concept - RabbitHole OS

**Concept:** Your entire digital life becomes one explorable knowledge graph. This is Vannevar Bush's 1945 vision, finally realized.

**Vision:**
Everything you do - read, watch, research, chat, write - automatically feeds into your personal knowledge graph.

**Features:**

### Core Integration
- **Browser extension:** Captures everything you read
  - Articles, papers, tweets, Reddit threads
  - Auto-extracts key concepts
  - Links to related explorations
- **Email integration:** Captures conversations
  - Identifies topics discussed
  - Links emails to relevant knowledge nodes
  - Creates "people" nodes with expertise tags
- **Calendar integration:** Shows what topics you explored when
  - "You researched AI safety during March 2024"
  - Time-based navigation
  - Correlate life events with learning
- **File system integration:** Auto-tag and connect all files
  - PDFs parsed for concepts
  - Images analyzed for content
  - Code repos linked to technical concepts
- **Note-taking integration:**
  - Obsidian bidirectional sync
  - Notion database sync
  - Apple Notes/OneNote capture
  - Roam Research graph merge
- **Chat integration:**
  - Slack/Discord messages become nodes
  - ChatGPT conversations imported
  - Meeting transcripts analyzed

### Search & Navigation
- **Concept-based search:** Not keywords, but ideas
  - "Show me everything I learned about machine learning in March 2024"
  - "Find all times I discussed consciousness with John"
  - "What was I reading when I had that breakthrough about quantum mechanics?"
- **Temporal navigation:** Travel through your intellectual history
- **Connection discovery:** "You read this article, then talked to Sarah, then had this idea"
- **Serendipity engine:** "Based on your graph, you might be interested in..."

### AI Features
- **Personal AI trained on YOUR graph**
  - Knows everything you've learned
  - Answers questions from your knowledge
  - Makes connections you haven't seen
  - Suggests explorations based on gaps
- **Auto-summarization:** Daily/weekly summaries of what you learned
- **Insight detection:** Flags when you're approaching a breakthrough
- **Contradiction finder:** Shows where your beliefs conflict
- **Knowledge decay alerts:** "You learned this 6 months ago, review it?"

### Visualization
- **Timeline view:** Your intellectual journey over time
- **Network view:** Concepts and how they connect
- **People view:** Who taught you what
- **Source view:** Which books/articles were most influential
- **Domain view:** Your expertise map

### Privacy & Security
- **Fully local-first:** Your graph stays on your devices
- **E2E encrypted cloud backup**
- **Selective sharing:** Share portions of your graph
- **Export everything:** No lock-in
